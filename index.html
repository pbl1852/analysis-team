01 · Introduction

Why This Analysis Matters

StreamKUS is a new media company preparing to release its first original content on YouTube and Netflix. Since audience preferences change quickly, relying only on intuition can be risky. Therefore, this project uses a data-driven approach to identify what kinds of genres, themes, and story elements appeal to viewers.

For this report, our team analyzed eight popular contents across four genre categories: romance, thriller/crime, human and social documentary drama, and comedy. The selected contents were Single’s Inferno and When Life Gives You Tangerines for romance, Squid Game and Parasyte for thriller, There Are No Bad Dogs in the World and The Trauma Code for slice of life, and SNL Korea and Shortbox for comedy. By comparing viewer reactions to these works, we aimed to discover patterns that can guide StreamKUS’s future content planning.

Genres Covered

We picked 4 different genres: romance, slice of life, thriller, and comedy, and picked 2 shows from each genre to analyze. The reason we divided our analysis targets into genres was because we would get a better understanding of what appeals more to what audience, and for what reason. We picked these 4 genres because they are historically among the most popular genres, and we thought that this would help us get a better idea for planning content.

Dramas: We used the most watched Netflix shows from 2024-25 from each genre: 1 romance, 1 slice of life, and 3 thriller. For Korean reviews, we used Watchapedia (pedia.watcha.com), and for English reviews, we used Rotten Tomatoes (rottentomatoes.com). For each show, we used Codex to collect all user reviews available without post filtering, and piled them into a CSV file.

Reality: We used 1 romance, 1 slice of life, and 2 comedy reality shows that gained traction in recent years, aired on Netflix, TV, YouTube, and Coupang Play respectively. We collected YouTube comments and posts on social media sites Nate (nate.com) and DCInside (dcinside.com), and piled them into a CSV file using Codex for analysis.

For dramas, we used audience reviews, not critic reviews, as we thought that the overall reviews of a wider audience would be more fit for our needs than the more professional critic reviews. The same thought process went into collecting reality show reviews, as we thought YouTube comments as well as social media posts would be able to represent a wider audience, therefore leading to a better understanding of what the wider audience yearns.

03 · Text Mining Methods

Analytical Techniques Used

Method 01

Sentiment Analysis

We collected the sentiment analysis of the collected text using Colab.

Method 02

AI-Assisted Review Analysis

Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite Preview was used to generate review summaries, key themes, positive points, negative points, and keyword lists.

Method 03

Genre-Based Strategy

We compared the main positive and negative points for each genre and show to identify what StreamKUS should do and avoid.

We collected the sentiment analysis of the collected text using Colab, and with Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite Preview we used the following prompt:

"당신은 영화/TV 평론가이자 데이터 분석가입니다.전체 리뷰 중 {sample_size}개를 무작위로 추출한 데이터를 바탕으로 종합적인 리뷰 분석을 작성해 주세요.요구사항:**주의사항: 모든 분석 결과와 응답값은 반드시 '한국어(Korean)'로 작성해 주세요.**. 이 작품에 대한 사람들의 반응과 점수 분석 근거를 한국어로 명확하게 작성하세요. 긍정적 피드백, 부정적 피드백, 반복되는 주제를 배열 형태로 매우 상세하게 요약하세요. **중요**: 각 항목은 반드시 '**주제:** 설명' 형식으로 작성하세요. (예시: '**세계관의 확장:** 단순히 게임을 반복하는 것이 아니라...'). 샘플링된 리뷰들에서 가장 많이 언급된 핵심 키워드 상위 10개를 추출하고, 해당 키워드가 샘플 리뷰 내에서 대략 몇 번 언급되었는지 빈도수를 추정하여 포함하세요. 결론을 한국어로 작성하세요."

The result printed the overall sentiment score out of 10, the frequency of each number, main positive and negative points, and the most frequently mentioned topics and keywords.

Because our end goal is proposing content for our streaming platform, we analyzed the main positive and negative points for each genre/show and used them to pinpoint what we need to do and what to avoid when making a show for each genre.

04 · Findings

What Viewers Responded To

💗
Target: Women in their 10s, 20s, and 40s

Romance

This genre appealed most to women in their 10s, 20s, and 40s. For dramas, a good cast, good characters, a touching storyline, and good videography were the positives that appeared most often. However, unnecessary scenes and an incomplete plot were frequently discussed as negative points.

For reality shows, the cast made the biggest difference, along with the chemistry between participants. Long scenes and poor editing were the main complaints.

🎭
Target: Men 20s, 30s, 50+ / Women 40s

Slice of Life

This genre appealed most to men in their 20s, 30s, and 50s or above, and women in their 40s. For dramas, a good plot, good characters, and a clear message were key positive factors. Unrealistic scenes were the main complaint.

For reality shows, educational aspects and touching scenes were the main positives. However, repetitive plots made some viewers feel bored. The main talking point was the message the content wanted to deliver.

🔍
Target: Men in their 10s, 20s, 40s, and 50+

Thriller

This genre appealed most to men in their 10s, 20s, 40s, and 50s or above. A creative storyline, good acting, and strong videography were the most frequently mentioned positive points.

However, illogical story consistency and underwhelming endings were the main complaints. These shows were also often compared to their predecessors or other similar titles.

😄
Target: Men 40s, 50+ / Women 40s

Comedy

This genre appealed most to men in their 40s and 50s or above, and women in their 40s. Easily understandable and relatable topics, along with social satire, made comedy shows more appealing.

However, unrealistic or exaggerated points, generalization, and too much political intent made these shows harder to watch. Comedy shows tended to have very mixed reviews, with various opinions coexisting.

05 · Content Proposals

Strategic Recommendations for StreamKUS

1

When The Seasons Stay
우리의 계절은 아직

Genre: Romance Target: Women 10s, 20s, 40s Format: 6-Part Netflix Series

StreamKUS proposes “When the Seasons Stay” as its first original emotional romance and family healing drama series. The story follows people who reconnect with love, family, and forgotten relationships while navigating the emotional changes of adulthood.

While many romance dramas focus mainly on excitement, confession, or dramatic heartbreak, this series explores the quieter emotions that remain afterward. Love is portrayed not as a perfect fantasy, but as something built slowly through understanding, forgiveness, sacrifice, and shared everyday moments.

Episodes

  1. Ep.1: The Place I Left Behind — Returning home and facing old memories
  2. Ep.2: Things We Never Said — Emotional distance within family
  3. Ep.3: That Rain That Day — Reuniting with a first love
  4. Ep.4: Dinner For Four — Family warmth hidden in everyday life
  5. Ep.5: After The Flowers Fall — Loss, regret, and emotional healing
  6. Ep.6: When The Seasons Stay — Accepting love, family, and moving forward

Reasons based on collected data: Text-mining analysis showed that viewers respond strongly to emotional humanism in Korean romance dramas. Keywords such as cried, love, family, and touching suggest that audiences value sincere stories about relationships, family bonds, healing, and meaningful human connections.

2

The Second Golden Time
두 번째 골든타임

Genre: Slice of Life Format: 6-Part Netflix Series + Short-Form Content

“The Second Golden Time” is a human and social documentary drama that focuses on what happens after a patient survives an emergency. Each episode begins with an urgent medical case, but instead of ending with a successful surgery, the story follows the patient’s second crisis after survival: medical bills, lack of caregivers, trauma, unemployment, family conflict, and social isolation.

The title refers to the crucial period after treatment, when a patient needs support to return to normal life. By focusing on this stage, the show can provide both tension and catharsis while delivering a realistic and empathetic social message.

Episodes

  1. Ep.1: Alive But Not — A lone elder has nowhere to go after discharge
  2. Ep.2: Discharge Faster Than Money — A delivery driver must return to work after an accident
  3. Ep.3: Lone Wolf — Successful surgery, but no one to help afterward
  4. Ep.4: Alive At What Cost — A family struggles to pay hospital bills
  5. Ep.5: Eerie at the ER — A deeper look into a misunderstood family member
  6. Ep.6: 72 Hours After Discharge — Following a patient after discharge and showing how healthcare and community support should connect

Reasons based on collected data: Viewers of The Trauma Code responded positively to fast pacing, charismatic characters, team chemistry, cathartic moments, and criticism of the medical system. However, some viewers criticized exaggerated scenes and unrealistic hero-like characters. This proposal keeps the strengths while focusing on more realistic problems after emergency treatment.

3

Dead Chamber
죽음의 룰렛

Genre: Thriller Target: Men 10s, 20s, 40s, 50+ Format: 6-Part Netflix Series

StreamKUS proposes “Dead Chamber” as its first original psychological survival thriller series. The story follows six strangers who are invited to participate in a mysterious roulette game where each spin determines life or death.

While many survival thrillers focus mainly on violence and action, this series emphasizes psychological fear, human instinct, betrayal, and moral conflict. Suspense comes not only from deadly games but also from the emotional pressure created when ordinary people are forced to make impossible choices.

Episodes

  1. Ep.1: The Invitation — Six strangers enter a mysterious roulette game
  2. Ep.2: The First Spin — The players realize the game is deadly
  3. Ep.3: Trust No One — Alliances form while suspicion grows
  4. Ep.4: Loaded Chamber — Hidden rules are revealed
  5. Ep.5: The Last Bet — Betrayal destroys the remaining survivors
  6. Ep.6: Dead Chamber — The truth behind the game is uncovered

Reasons based on collected data: Survival thrillers such as Squid Game and Parasyte have attracted strong audience attention. Review analysis showed that keywords such as immersion, visual excitement, video quality, and CG appeared frequently in thriller discussions. This suggests that viewers value cinematic tension, high-quality visuals, and immersive survival mechanics.

4

We’re Not Done Yet
우리 아직 안 끝났어

Genre: Comedy Target: 20s and 30s with high media usage Format: YouTube + Netflix Expansion Potential

This show follows a couple who got married after seven years of dating but run into realistic fights and laughter as newlyweds. Unlike shows that focus on dating, breakup, or marriage as the ending, this series focuses on life after marriage.

The show is centered on everyday situations such as dates after marriage, housewarming, in-laws, money, work, and social life. These familiar situations are presented in a comedic way so that viewers can easily relate to them.

Episodes

  1. Ep.1: Do We Need Dates? We’re Married! — Boundaries between dating and marriage
  2. Ep.2: The Housewarming War — Friends, housewarming, and appearances
  3. Ep.3: Your Mother, Not Mine! — In-law problems
  4. Ep.4: Your Money? Our Money? — Money management problems
  5. Ep.5: You’re So Stuck In The Past — Repetitive talk about past experiences
  6. Ep.6: You’ve Got Company — Social life after marriage

Reasons based on collected data: YouTube comments and social media posts about Shortbox showed that viewers react strongly to relatable comedy based on everyday life. Keywords such as newlywed, military service, work, and marriage appeared often. Because some viewers also used negative words such as boring, repetitive, and stop, this proposal chooses the less common topic of life after marriage and leaves room for future episode topics to be decided through viewer comments.

06 · Limitations

Research Constraints

⚠️

Limited Data Scope

The analysis was based on selected contents and available online comments, so the findings may not fully represent every viewer group.

⚠️

Platform Bias

Viewer reactions may differ depending on the platform. YouTube comments, Watchapedia reviews, Rotten Tomatoes reviews, and community posts may each reflect different user groups.

⚠️

Interpretation Limits

Some reactions may include sarcasm, exaggeration, or context-dependent meanings that are difficult to fully capture through text mining alone.

07 · Conclusion

Key Takeaways

This project analyzed viewer reactions to eight popular contents across four genre categories. The analysis showed that each genre appeals to audiences through different emotional and narrative elements. Romance content depends strongly on emotional immersion, chemistry, and sincere human relationships. Slice of life content works well when it delivers empathy, realism, education, and a clear social message. Thriller content attracts viewers through creative storylines, visual immersion, suspense, and strong production quality. Comedy content becomes effective when it uses relatable situations, everyday humor, and social satire while avoiding excessive exaggeration or repetition.

Based on these findings, StreamKUS should develop content that clearly delivers emotional value to viewers. The proposed ideas are an emotional romance drama, a realistic human and social drama, a psychological survival thriller, and a short-form comedy series with expansion potential. However, this analysis has limitations because the selected contents and data sources were limited. With more time, future research could collect more comments from additional platforms and compare audience reactions by age group, gender, country, and platform.
